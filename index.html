Hi, I'm Amanda.


I received my MS in Data Science in December 2020 from Columbia University, where I worked on research projects for professors in Management, Operations Research, and Biomedical Informatics. While completing my Master's, I also spent a summer at American Express working on their Finance Decision Science team to forecast card fee revenue for small businesses in the US. Prior to that, I was a quantitative analyst and Data Science Fellow at Abt Associates, a policy research and evaluation firm, where I contributed to projects related to workforce development, housing, and education.

I graduated in June 2017 from the University of Chicago with a BA in Political Science and a minor in Statistics. While I have worked with data related to a variety of fields, the common theme among the projects that interest me most are simply ones where I get to work on interesting problems and affect positive social change. My primary research interests are, from a Data Science perspective, Natural Language Processing, and, from a domain perspective, organizational behavior.

Projects

MovieLens Hybrid Recommender Sysyem

Fall 2020

Created end-to-end framework for producing a Top 10 list of movies for individual users using the MovieLens dataset by building a hybrid recommender system that utilizes a parallel ensemble switching method. Scraped movie data from Wikipedia to construct a content-based algorithm that uses tree-based Approximate Nearest Neighbors (ANN) to find similar movies for users. After comparing several hybrid systems across novelty, precision, and recall, our team's final product was a hybrid system using item-based collaborative filtering and autoencoders to make recommendations.


Are Small Business Closures Curbing the Spread of COVID-19?

Spring 2020

Combined data from a local business scheduling tool with coronavirus case data and Census data to perform an iterative analysis on the effects of small business closures on coronavirus case rates in cities by [1] attempting to cluster cities based on case rates on each day and [2] regressing lagged case rates on small business closures on those results. Selected as a finalist for Columbia Data Science Institute’s Center for Health Analytics COVID-19 Data Challenge.


Exploring Prescription Painkiller Use and Abuse

Fall 2019

Designed research questions related to factors correlated with opioid misuse and performed exploratory data analysis as well as created visualizations to answer these questions using R and d3.
